Information Technology

In the past few decades there has been a revolution in computing and communications,and all indications are that technological progress and use of information technology will continue.The revolution in information and communication technology has changed not only our lives but also the way how people do business.

Using information technology, companies possess the potential to reach more customers, introduce new products and services quickly, and collaborate with suppliers and business partners from all over the world.Transformation from industrial society to information society and industrial economy to knowledge economy is a result of the impact of ICT and Internet use.

Using ICT also facilitates the production of goods in a short time with the help of computerized information systems, and services are quick and efective (Miles, 2001, 232). Information and Communication Technology known as ICT technology has become the main tool in business activities in the modern world (Dimovski and Å kerlavaj, 2004, 636).

Definition of terms:

Computing
- the use or operation of computers.
Communication
- means of sending or receiving information, such as phone lines or computers.
Information technology
- is the use of computers to store, retrieve, transmit, and manipulate data or information.
Revolution
- the period made by the regular succession of a measure of time or by a succession of similar events.
Business
- is the activity of making one's living or making money by producing or buying and selling products (such as goods and services).
Potential
- having or showing the capacity to become or develop into something in the future.
Services
- the action of helping or doing work for someone.
Collaborate
- to work together or with someone else for a special purpose.
Indutrial Society
- is a society driven by the use of technology to enable mass production, supporting a large population with a high capacity for division of labour.
Economy
- the wealth and resources of a country or region, especially in terms of the production and consumption of goods and services.
